GENERAL INFO

Calcite is a carbonate mineral with the chemical formula CaCO3. It is one of the most common minerals on Earth and can be found in a wide variety of geological settings. Calcite is a major component of limestone, which makes up about 10% of the Earth's sedimentary rocks.

Physical Properties:


  • Color: Calcite can be colorless, white, or various shades of gray, yellow, green, blue, brown, or red, depending on impurities present during its formation.
  • Crystal System: It crystallizes in the trigonal crystal system, typically forming rhombohedral or scalenohedral crystals.
  • Hardness: It has a Mohs hardness of 3, making it relatively soft.
  • Transparency: Calcite can be transparent to translucent.
